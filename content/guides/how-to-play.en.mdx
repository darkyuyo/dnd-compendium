# How to play

This guide summarizes the basic flow of a **Dungeons & Dragons** session for your table. Replace or expand it with text from your Player's Handbook when you extract the PDF.

## The table

- A **Dungeon Master (DM)** narrates the world, plays NPCs, and adjudicates rules.
- **Players** each control one character and describe what they try to do.

## Adventure loop

1. The DM describes the scene.
2. Players say what their characters do.
3. The DM calls for rolls when the outcome is uncertain (ability checks, attacks, saves).
4. The DM narrates the result and the scene continues.

## Combat (summary)

1. **Initiative**: each combatant rolls 1d20 + Dexterity modifier.
2. On your turn you can move and take **one action** (Attack, Cast a Spell, Dodge, Help, etc.).
3. Many classes also have **bonus actions** or **reactions**.
4. Combat ends when one side flees, surrenders, or falls.

## Key rolls

| Roll | When |
|------|------|
| Ability check | Doing something with an uncertain outcome |
| Attack roll | Hitting with a weapon or attack spell |
| Saving throw | Resisting a dangerous effect |

## Rests

- **Short rest**: recover some resources (e.g. Second Wind).
- **Long rest**: regain hit points, spell slots, and most features.
